Seats that
sold
themselves.

What I did

Campaign Concept · Creative Direction · Integrated Execution


Show messy thinking
What's the one thing we can offer online that can't be offered offline? Hmmmm, selecting seats… but they can select offline… but the ticket counter guy will be pissed if one guy overthinks a seat… the customer has too many thoughts in their head… best view… na, comfort… wouldn't it be great if seats themselves announced their special-ness??! WHOA! Yeah, what if seats talk for themselves?! Stop it! Don't anthropomorphise the seats… But it'll look sooo good, the animation, imagine aisle seat running across… stop! Imagine… Let's see the data first! OMG. Data agrees. We're doing this. How to convince the CMO now… Will she agree on giving seats a personality?!

While people love watching movies any day of the week, they don't really book them online—on BookMyShow. Weekday sales remained stagnant, making urgency-based marketing ineffective.

So we tried solving the problem by looking at booking patterns, and found something simple—the seat people watch a movie from matters. A lot.

People have strong (and very personal) seat preferences depending on who they're going with, what they're watching, and the kind of experience they want. But when booking last-minute at the counter, they usually end up compromising.

So we solved our problem by solving theirs. By highlighting BestSeller Seats, a feature that helps people identify the most preferred seats. And why are they the most preferred seats? We let the seats do the talking!

From conceptualising the idea to leading its execution, I spearheaded the integrated campaign across platforms, increasing weekday bookings by 20%.

to hospitals.

Campaign Concept · Film Narrative


Show messy thinking
Everyone's doing something for COVID, we should do something too… something nostalgic? Make them realise how good they had it with us… no! Everything is anyway gloomy… Don't, that'll be hitting the raw nerve… ugh, Christmas is around… can we do something around that? Someone said we can be elves who bring good news… but there's no good news… everything is super sensitive… hmmm… we can't be elves… but we can dress people who already are… what? 2020 wasn't exactly the year for Christmas cheer.

Hospitals were overwhelmed, and the people holding it all together barely had a moment to pause.

In the middle of all of that, the BookMyShow creative team and I worked with the CSR wing to conceptualise #LoveBoostsHealth—a campaign where we sent Santa- and elf-themed PPE kits to hospitals, to bring a little unexpected cheer to both healthcare providers and patients.

The campaign came alive through a 90-second film that travelled across YouTube, social media, and press—striking a chord at a time when optimism was in short supply.

Ahh, this can be the next Netflix… nice project to work on… but no, this model won't work in India, people are misers… but that's the brief, we oughta make it work… okay… let's see what Stream and BMS will have in common? Hmm, tickets, theatre-like feel, fdfs thrills, will it make it stand out? Let the marketers decide that; our copy is doing the job as per the brief!

The pandemic was brutal. We were all stuck in a non-stop scroll cycle, watching anything and everything, but nothing was quite like going to the movies.

That's when BookMyShow, India's biggest entertainment platform, decided to launch Stream, bringing cinema to people's homes! Owing to years of rich consumer data, they curated 700+ titles of world cinema for their movie-loving audience across every demographic.

The creative challenge was to give Stream a distinctive tone of voice that could guide choice while still giving BookMyShow feels.

So that's what I did. Along with a team of freelance writers, I created the end-to-end tone of voice—from the website to social media communications, I crafted all the launch messaging.

By day, I conjure branded stories. By night, I wander through art galleries, books, movies, Substacks, and practice ceramics.
